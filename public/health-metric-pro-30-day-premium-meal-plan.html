How to use this plan

This plan is designed to remove daily decision fatigue without making food feel rigid. Follow it closely when you want structure, or use the swaps and portion guide to adapt meals around appetite, training, budget and preference.

1. Choose your portion size Use the portion guide below rather than forcing exact calories. Start moderate, then adjust based on hunger, energy and progress.
2. Shop once or twice per week Each weekly list is grouped by supermarket aisle so it is easier to scan on mobile.
3. Prep the small things Cook one protein, one carbohydrate and one tray of vegetables ahead where possible. Keep sauces simple for flavour without fuss.

Flexible portions

Simple plate guide

Use this as a practical starting point. If your goal is fat loss, begin with the lower end of the carbohydrate and fat portions. If your goal is performance or muscle gain, add a little more carbohydrate around training.

Protein 1 to 2 palm-sized portions per main meal. Examples: chicken, turkey, salmon, tuna, eggs, Greek yoghurt, tofu, beans or lentils.
Carbohydrate 1 fist-sized portion at most meals. Examples: oats, potatoes, rice, pasta, wraps, couscous or wholemeal bread.
Vegetables and fibre Aim for 2 fist-sized portions of vegetables or salad at lunch and dinner where possible. Add fruit, beans, oats and wholegrains for extra fibre.

This is general wellness guidance, not medical advice. Speak with a GP, registered dietitian or qualified clinician before changing your diet if you have a medical condition, are pregnant or breastfeeding, take medication, have allergies, or have a history of disordered eating.

Meal prep

Low-effort prep system

You do not need a full Sunday batch-cook to succeed. Prep the pieces that reduce friction: washed salad, cooked protein, ready carbohydrates and two simple sauces.

1

Wash and store produce

Wash salad leaves, chop cucumber and peppers, and keep fruit visible. The easier option usually wins.

2

Cook one flexible protein

Roast chicken, turkey mince, tofu, lentils or boiled eggs can become wraps, bowls, salads or leftovers.

3

Prepare a simple carbohydrate

Cook rice, potatoes, pasta or couscous. Cool leftovers quickly and store safely in the fridge.

4

Keep flavour ready

Use lemon, herbs, salsa, yoghurt dressing, hummus, light pesto or reduced-salt stock to keep meals enjoyable.

Use these swaps when ingredients are out of stock, budget changes, or you want a vegetarian option.

Consistency tips

Make the plan easier to finish

Hydration reminder Keep a water bottle visible. Tea, coffee, lower-fat milk and sugar-free drinks can also contribute to fluid intake.
Progress marker Track energy, hunger, digestion, sleep and consistency. Weight is only one data point and can change day to day.
Busy day rule If the planned meal feels too hard, build a simple plate: protein, carbohydrate, vegetables and flavour. That still counts.
